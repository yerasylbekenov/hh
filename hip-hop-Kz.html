  • Hip-Hop in KZ

In the early 2000s, new rap groups began to appear in the country: Ghetto Dogs, DMH, Black Cost, Buhar Jerreau, HIRO and many others. Entire clans and rap schools were formed in the regions of Kazakhstan. The voice of the streets was heard in almost every courtyard. In 2009, the Da Gudda Jazz group was born, which combined in its music a variety of genres - from rap and reggae to rhythm and blues. At the same time, the then unknown Scryptonite appeared in the industry, which brought Kazakh hip-hop to a new level, adding a musical style and the complexity of music construction to it.
